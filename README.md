# 📉 Customer Churn Analysis

## 🎯 Objective

This project analyses customer data to identify the key drivers of churn and uncover patterns that can help improve customer retention. The goal is to turn data into actionable insights that support smarter business decisions and reduce customer attrition.

## 🔍 Key Findings

- **📅 Contract type matters:** Customers on month-to-month contracts are much more likely to churn than those on longer-term plans.
- **🛠️ Support is a strong signal:** Customers with higher support calls show a much higher risk of churn, making support access and efficiency an important retention factor.
- **👥 Age group patterns:** The 55–66 age group shows the highest churn rate, highlighting a major retention risk in this segment.
- **🚻 Gender differences exist:** Female customers consistently show higher churn rates across age groups.
- **💳 Payment behavior matters:** Longer payment delays are associated with higher churn, suggesting billing experience may influence retention.
- **💰 Value matters too:** Customers who stay tend to have higher total spend, reinforcing the importance of retaining higher-value accounts.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

- **Language:** Python
- **Environment:** Anaconda
- **Libraries:** Pandas for data cleaning, Seaborn and Matplotlib for visualization, and Scikit-learn for modeling

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Prioritize month-to-month customers with targeted retention campaigns.
- Improve support experiences for customers showing early signs of churn.
- Focus retention efforts on the 55–66 age segment.
- Monitor payment delays and billing-related issues more closely.
- Strengthen retention strategies for high-value customers.

## 🚀 Conclusion

This analysis shows that churn is influenced by a mix of contract type, support usage, payment behavior, age, and customer value. These insights can help the business identify high-risk customers earlier and take proactive steps to improve retention and protect revenue.
